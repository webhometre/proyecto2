FTA (Failure to Appear): In a legal context, FTA stands for "Failure to Appear." What is it? It's the charge filed when a person has been formally summoned by a judge to appear in court on a specific date and time and fails to do so. Consequences: Bench Warrant: The judge will immediately issue an arrest warrant for the person. Forfeiture of Bail: If the person was free on bail, the judge may order the forfeiture of the bail money. Additional Charge: The failure to appear can be prosecuted as a separate offense, adding an extra layer of legal complexity to the original case..

Contempt of Court (COC) Contempt of Court is a legal charge invoked when a person shows disrespect, defies the authority of a court, or willfully disobeys a court order. In the U.S. justice system, the court is the highest authority in a proceeding; therefore, any action that obstructs, interferes with, or ignores a judge's order is considered a serious offense not only against the opposing party (if any) but also against the judicial system itself. Classification of Contempt Contempt of Court can be classified in two ways, depending on the judge's intent: 1. Civil Contempt: Its purpose is coercive. It is used to compel a person to comply with a specific order (such as turning over documents, paying alimony, or appearing in court). The judge imposes sanctions (such as jail time or daily fines) that continue until the person decides to comply with the order. 2. Criminal Contempt: Its purpose is punitive. It is used to punish conduct that has already occurred and that was a direct offense against the dignity or authority of the court (for example, yelling at the judge or deliberately ignoring a subpoena)..
